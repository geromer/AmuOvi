AmuOvi Easy Cart

Grocery shopping, made calm.
Read time ~12 minutes · Updated for current build

AmuOvi Easy Cart is a calm, private, offline-first grocery companion. It turns shopping into three simple acts — Plan, Shop, Review — and quietly learns from your habits to help you spend less without thinking about it.

No account. No cloud. No tracking. Everything lives on your phone.

At a glance

The features that make Easy Cart different from a plain shopping list:

📷

Offline barcode scanning

EAN, UPC, QR. Works in basement aisles with no signal. Names auto-fill when you're back online.

⚠️

Price warnings

Tells you when you're paying more than your own historical low — and which store had it cheaper.

🔔

Smart restock reminders

Detects buying patterns (milk every 7 days, coffee every 21) and schedules local notifications.

📊

Spending insights

Trend chart, category donut, store reports, and one "quiet observation" each visit with a specific dollar recommendation.

📈

Per-item price chart

Tap any history row to see how that product's price has moved over time across stores.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family sharing

Share your reminder list via any messaging app. The link auto-imports into the recipient's Easy Cart.

📡

WiFi sync

Copy your data between two phones over local WiFi. No cloud involved.

📄

CSV export

Every purchase exportable as a spreadsheet for budgeting apps, Numbers, or Excel.

🔒

Fully private

SQLite on your device. No account, no analytics, no third-party SDKs. Uninstall = data gone.

This guide walks you through every feature — from your first scan to advanced flows like WiFi sync, deep-link import, and Spending Insights with category breakdowns. Skim the table of contents below or read straight through; sections are independent.

Read this first — give it 2 to 3 weeks.

Easy Cart gets noticeably smarter as you use it. The first time you scan something, you'll type the name, price, and category yourself. From your second purchase onward, the same product auto-fills everything — name, last price, quantity, unit, category, and even the cheapest store you bought it at. After 2 or 3 weeks of regular shopping, most of your basket fills itself.

What "gets smarter" actually means

These features only work once you have history. The more trips you log, the more value they deliver:

FeatureNeeds to work properly
📷 Name auto-fill on scan At least 1 prior scan of the same barcode. After that, scanning is instant — no typing.
⌨️ Type-ahead suggestions At least a handful of manual entries. Once you've added "bananas" once, typing "ba" suggests it next time with everything pre-filled.
⚠️ Price warnings You need to have bought the same item at 2+ different prices (or stores). Easy Cart compares against your own historical low.
🔔 Restock pattern detection At least 3-4 purchases of the same item, spaced out. Then Easy Cart can suggest "you buy this every ~7 days."
📊 Spending insights & trends At least 2 weeks of trips for the weekly view; 2 months for the monthly chart to feel meaningful.
📈 Per-item price chart At least 2 purchases of the same item over time.
✨ "Quiet observation" recommendation Trips at 2+ different stores, a few times each. Easy Cart compares your real basket costs.
🏷️ Category breakdown donut Categorize items as you add them. After a couple weeks, the donut reflects your real spending shape.

Tips to bootstrap faster

Bootstrap tip

Categorize from the start.

It only takes one extra tap per item, but the insights donut needs categorized data to be useful. Start clean, thank yourself later.

Bootstrap tip

Don't skip the first trips.

The first 3-4 trips feel like more work than a plain list — that's normal. You're teaching the app. By trip 5, scanning becomes scrolling.

Bootstrap tip

Always save the store name.

The "cheaper at X" feature and the cheapest-store insight both depend on having store names attached to trips. Type it even if you're tired.

Bootstrap tip

Edit weird scans early.

If a barcode shows the wrong name (cross-brand match in Open Food Facts), tap the pencil and fix it once — the correction sticks forever.

In short: the first two weeks are an investment. After that, Easy Cart starts working for you instead of with you.

01Quick start

Three steps to go from "just installed" to "first trip saved."

  1. Tap the blue scan button (bottom right)Point your camera at a product barcode. Easy Cart reads it instantly — works fully offline.
  2. Confirm the itemThe product name, quantity, unit, price, and category are pre-filled when possible. Edit as needed, then tap Add to Cart.
  3. When you're done shopping, tap DoneEnter (or pick) the store name. Easy Cart saves the trip and shows you a quick summary of how it compared to your usual spend at that store.
Don't have a barcode? Tap the gold + button next to the scan button to enter an item manually. Perfect for produce, bakery, and bulk items.

02The main screen

Everything lives on one screen. Tap a section heading to expand or collapse it — only one section is open at a time, so the view stays calm.

🛒

Current Grocery Cart

What you're shopping for right now. Shows your running total and a weekly spending bar comparing this week's spend to last week's. Tap Done when you check out.

🔔

Shopping Reminder

Items you want to remember for next time, grouped by store. Any Restock Reminders that are due appear here too, in a separate group. Tap the checkbox while shopping to mark items as bought — they're auto-removed when you tap Done.

📊

Spending Insights

A hero number with a delta vs. your average, a 6-period trend chart you can toggle Week / Month / Year, a "quiet observation" with a specific recommendation, a category breakdown, and per-store reports. Updated automatically after every trip.

🕓

History

Every item you've ever bought, searchable by name or store. Tap a row to see a price-over-time chart for that item. Tap the bell icon to set a restock reminder, the pencil to edit, or the trash to delete. Tap CSV in the header to export everything as a spreadsheet.

The cloud icon in the top right Opens WiFi Sync — copy your whole purchase history between two phones on the same local network. See section 12.

03Adding items

Three ways to put something into your cart.

Scan a barcode

Tap the blue scan button. Point your camera at any barcode — EAN-13, UPC-A, or QR. Easy Cart recognizes it instantly. If you've scanned this product before, the name and category appear automatically. If not, Easy Cart tries to look it up online via Open Food Facts (when an internet connection is available).

Enter manually

Tap the gold + button. The same form opens, just empty. Type the item name and Easy Cart will auto-suggest past items as you type — tap a suggestion to fill the whole form (name, quantity, unit, price, category) in one tap. Otherwise fill the fields yourself.

New: Name suggestions Typing "milk" in the Add Item modal shows every past item containing "milk" with its last price, quantity, unit, and store. One tap pre-fills everything — including the right category.

Pick up something already on your Reminder list

If an item is on your Shopping Reminder, you still need to scan or manually add it when you actually buy it — that way the price-warning logic works. Tap the checkbox in the reminder row to mark it as bought, and it'll be removed automatically after you save the trip.

Units that compare correctly Easy Cart only compares prices across the same unit family — weight (kg/g), volume (L/ml), or count (qty). It won't tell you a kilo of apples is "cheaper" than a liter of milk.

04Categories

Every item belongs to a category. Categories power the donut chart in Spending Insights.

When you add an item, a Category selector appears below the price field. Tap one of the pills to set the category — defaults to General if you don't change it. For scanned items, Easy Cart can sometimes auto-detect (e.g. products with "tomato" in the name go to Vegetable).

Available categories:

CategoryExamples
GeneralDefault · anything that doesn't fit below
ProduceVegetables, fruits, fresh herbs
DairyMilk, cheese, yogurt, butter
MeatBeef, chicken, fish, deli
PantryOils, grains, canned goods, condiments

Why categorize? Once you have a few weeks of categorized data, Spending Insights can tell you things like "42% of your spend went to produce this month" or "snacks doubled since February."

05The cart & price warnings

The cart is your in-progress trip. It's cleared automatically when you tap Done.

For every item you add, Easy Cart quietly checks your own purchase history for the lowest price you've ever paid for that same item (normalized to per-kilogram, per-liter, or per-unit). If you're paying more this time, the cart row turns gold and shows you where you got it cheaper:

Whole Milk · 4.50
⚠ cheaper at Costco: $3.79/L

This isn't a guess. It's based on what you have actually paid, at the same unit, in the past. The store name is the one where the cheapest historical price was recorded.

Fix Names button

If you scanned items offline and they show as raw barcode numbers (e.g. 0049000050202), a Fix Names button appears in the cart header. Tap it once you're back online — Easy Cart looks up every unresolved name in one batch via Open Food Facts.

Edit & remove

Each cart row has a pencil (edit) and a trash (delete) icon. Edits update only the cart — historical purchases of the same item are unchanged.

06Saving a trip & trip summary

Tap Done when you're at the checkout.

Easy Cart asks "Where did you shop?". Recent stores appear as chips you can tap. Type a new name if it's a first visit. Tap Confirm & Save Trip and:

  1. A trip record is createdLinking the store name to today's date.
  2. Every cart item becomes a history entryPermanent, searchable, exportable.
  3. The cart is clearedReady for the next shop.
  4. Checked reminders are deletedItems you ticked off during the trip are removed from Shopping Reminder automatically.
  5. You see a trip summaryA modal pops up showing today's total vs your typical spend at this store, with a delta chip. Tap to dismiss.
New: Post-trip summary The summary modal tells you whether this trip was higher, lower, or about the same as your average for that store — useful for catching unusually expensive visits while they're still fresh in mind.

07Shopping reminders

Your "buy next time" list. Grouped by store.

The Shopping Reminder section lives between Cart and Spending Insights. Tap the + button on the right of its heading to add items. A modal appears with:

  • A search bar across all items you've ever bought
  • A list you can tap-select (checkboxes appear)
  • A manual-entry field at the bottom for anything new

Selected items are added to your Reminder. Easy Cart remembers the last price and the last store for each, so you can plan your route — items are automatically grouped by store in the section.

Working through the list in-store

Each reminder row has a checkbox. Tap it once you've put the item in your physical cart — the row turns green with strikethrough. This is only for tracking. You still need to scan or manually add the item to your Easy Cart so the price warning works.

Cleanup is automatic

When you tap Done at checkout, all checked reminders are deleted. Unchecked reminders stay for next time.

08Restock reminders

Get a nudge before you run out.

Some items have a natural cadence — milk every 7 days, coffee beans every 21, vitamins every 30. Easy Cart watches your purchase history and predicts when you'll next need each item.

Setting one up

In your History section, tap the bell icon on any item row. A modal opens with:

  • A pattern badge showing the cadence Easy Cart detected (e.g. You buy this every ~7d)
  • Preset buttons: 3d · 7d · 14d · 30d
  • A free-text field if you want a custom interval

Tap Set Reminder. Easy Cart schedules a local notification for that future date.

When the date arrives

You get a notification. The item also appears in your Shopping Reminder section under "Upcoming Restocks" with a colored badge:

  • in 5 days · approaching
  • in 2 days · urgent
  • Overdue · the predicted date has passed

Acting on a restock reminder

Each restock row has a paper-plane icon — tap it to add the item directly to your cart (price warning applies). Or hit the trash icon to dismiss the reminder.

09Spending insights

Where your grocery money actually goes — told as a story, not a wall of numbers.

Tap the Spending Insights heading to expand. You'll see these blocks, top-to-bottom:

1. Hero number + trend chart

A big serif total (e.g. $268.40) with a delta chip showing how it compares to your average for that period (↓ 8% means you're spending less). Below it, a 6-bar trend chart with the current period highlighted. Toggle Week / Month / Year to change the timeframe.

2. Quiet observation

One specific, actionable sentence — only shown when Easy Cart has enough data to make a real recommendation. For example:

"Your trips at Walmart average $54 — at Aldi you average $31. Shifting some shopping could save about $46/month."

3. Category breakdown

A donut chart showing how your spend splits across Produce, Dairy, Meat, Pantry, Bakery, Snacks, Household, and General. Each slice is labelled with its percentage. Period follows the Week / Month / Year toggle above.

4. Quick-stats grid

5. Store reports

A per-store breakdown of total spend, trip count, and average per trip. Useful for spotting which stores actually save you money once you've shopped at both a few times.

6. Recent trips list

Your most recent shopping trips with store, date, total, and item count.

10History, search & price chart

Everything you've ever bought, searchable, with a price chart for every item.

The History section shows your purchases newest-first. Each row tells you the item, quantity, unit, price, date, and store. Four touch targets:

  • Tap the row itself — opens a Price History modal showing a chart of how this item's price has changed over time, plus a list of every past purchase.
  • Bell — set a restock reminder for this item
  • Pencil — edit the entry (useful for fixing wrong scans)
  • Trash — delete this single purchase
New: Tap a row to see the price chart Watch how a single item's price has trended across stores over months. Great for spotting inflation on your own basket or finding the best store for a specific item.

Search

When History is expanded, a search bar appears at the top. Type any part of an item name or store name — results filter as you type. Tap the X to reset.

Load more

History loads 50 entries at a time. Scroll to the bottom and tap Load more to fetch the next page.

11CSV export

Take your data anywhere.

In the History section header, tap the CSV button. Easy Cart writes a file named purchase_history_2026-05-24.csv with columns:

Date, Item, Quantity, Unit, Total Price, Price/Unit, Store

Then it opens your system share sheet so you can:

  • Save to Files / Drive / Dropbox
  • Email it to yourself
  • Open it in Numbers / Excel / Google Sheets
  • Send to a budgeting app
Per-unit price is calculated for you The Price/Unit column makes spreadsheet analysis easier — sort by it to find your best deals on any single item.

12WiFi sync

Copy your data between two phones on the same network.

Tap the cloud icon in the top right corner. A modal lets you pick a mode:

Host (send) mode

Your phone becomes a temporary server. Tap Start Server, then check your phone's WiFi settings to find your IP address (something like 192.168.1.42). Share that IP with the second phone — read it aloud, text it, whatever.

Join (receive) mode

On the second phone, switch to Join, type the host's IP address, and tap Connect & Sync. The two phones sync over your local WiFi. A live log shows progress.

Privacy reminder Sync only works between phones on the same local network. No data leaves your home WiFi. There is no Easy Cart server in the cloud.

13Family sharing & deep-link import

Send your shopping reminder to whoever's doing the run — and they can import it with one tap.

In the Shopping Reminder section, tap the share icon in the header. Easy Cart opens your phone's share sheet with a clean message:

Shopping Reminder:
• Eggs (organic, dozen)
• Olive Oil 1L
• Sourdough Loaf

Import into EasyCart:
easycart://import?items=Eggs%20(organic%2C%20dozen),Olive%20Oil%201L,Sourdough%20Loaf

Pick WhatsApp / Messages / Mail / whatever. The recipient sees the items as plain text — and, if they also have Easy Cart installed, the special easycart:// link is tappable. Tapping it opens their app with a confirmation prompt to import your list.

Deep-link import flow When the recipient taps the link, Easy Cart shows a confirmation modal with each item listed. They tap Add to Shopping Reminder — duplicates are skipped silently, and the Reminder section auto-expands so they can see what was added.

14Tips & shortcuts

Little habits that make Easy Cart feel effortless.

Tip

Type before you scan.

If you've bought the item before, typing two letters of its name in the Add Item modal will surface it with all fields pre-filled. Often faster than scanning.

Tip

Group restock dates.

Set restock reminders for similar items (e.g. all your dry goods) on the same day, so you batch them into one trip.

Tip

Use Fix Names later.

If you're in a basement aisle with no signal, just scan everything. Tap Fix Names when you get reception. Saves checkout time.

Tip

Tap the row, not the buttons.

In History, tapping anywhere on the item text opens its price chart. Tap the icons on the right only when you want to edit or set a reminder.

Tip

Switch periods.

The Week / Month / Year toggle in Spending Insights changes the chart and the category donut. Use Year to see seasonal patterns; Week to react to recent overspend.

Tip

Search by store.

Typing "Costco" in History search shows just your Costco purchases — a fast way to verify if a "deal" was actually a deal.

15Privacy & your data

Everything stays on your phone unless you decide otherwise.

Easy Cart is built on three principles:

🔒No account. No sign-up, no email, no password. You open the app and use it.
📵No cloud. Your purchase history, prices, store list — all stored locally in a SQLite database on your phone. Easy Cart has no servers.
📡No tracking. No analytics, no advertising IDs, no third-party SDKs.

The only network calls Easy Cart makes are:

  • Open Food Facts API — only when you scan an unknown barcode. The API receives only the barcode number. (Skipped entirely if you're offline.)
  • WiFi Sync — only over your local network, only when you start it, only to a phone you've personally connected to.
  • System share sheet — whatever you choose (Messages, Mail, etc.). Easy Cart just hands the text off.

To wipe everything: uninstall the app. There's no "backup to cloud" that needs clearing — there was no cloud copy to begin with.

16Troubleshooting

Quick fixes for the most common snags.

ProblemTry this
Scanner shows a black screen Camera permission was denied. Open phone Settings → AmuOvi Easy Cart → Permissions → Camera → Allow.
Item name shows as a barcode number It's a new product not in your history and you were offline. Either type the name in the Add Item modal, or tap Fix Names in the cart header once you're back online.
Price warning didn't appear The cheapest price compared is normalized per unit. If you bought milk in different unit groups before (e.g. L vs qty), they don't compare. Edit historical entries to match units.
Restock notification didn't arrive Notification permission may be off. Phone Settings → AmuOvi Easy Cart → Notifications → Allow. Some Android battery savers also delay notifications — whitelist the app.
WiFi sync won't connect Both phones must be on the same network. Public WiFi often blocks phone-to-phone traffic — try your home network. Some Android phones also need "Local network" permission granted.
CSV export does nothing No purchase history yet — there's nothing to export. Save at least one trip first.
Deep-link import shows nothing The sender used an old format. Ask them to re-share from the current version, or paste the text and add items manually.
Category donut is missing You need at least a few categorized purchases. Older items without categories show as "General" — edit them to add proper categories.
"Quiet observation" never shows up Easy Cart only surfaces an observation when there's a meaningful difference between stores (>$5/trip and >$10/month projected savings). Shop at two stores a few times each and it'll appear.
Still stuck? Email the app maintainer with a brief description of what you tried. Include your phone OS version (iOS 17 / Android 14, etc.) and which feature is failing.
